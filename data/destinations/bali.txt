Bali, Indonesia - Complete Travel Guide

Overview:
Bali is a tropical paradise known for stunning beaches, ancient temples, lush rice terraces, and vibrant culture. Perfect for surfers, yoga enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

Best Time to Visit:
April to October (dry season). Avoid December-January (peak tourist season, high prices).

Budget Breakdown:
- Budget traveler: $30-50/day (hostels, local warungs, scooter rental)
- Mid-range: $80-150/day (decent hotels, mix of local and tourist restaurants)
- Luxury: $200+/day (resorts, fine dining, private tours)

Top Attractions:
- Uluwatu Temple: Clifftop temple with sunset Kecak dance performances ($5 entry)
- Tegalalang Rice Terraces: Instagram-famous green landscapes ($2 entry)
- Sacred Monkey Forest Ubud: Interact with monkeys in temple complex ($7 entry)
- Tanah Lot: Ocean temple, best at sunset ($3 entry)

Best Beaches:
- Seminyak: Upscale beach clubs, shopping, nightlife
- Canggu: Surfer paradise, digital nomad hub, trendy cafes
- Uluwatu: World-class surf breaks, dramatic cliffs
- Nusa Dua: Calm waters, family-friendly resorts
- Sanur: Peaceful, sunrise views, less crowded

Activities:
- Surfing lessons: $25-40 for 2 hours
- Scuba diving in Nusa Penida: $80-120 per dive
- Mount Batur sunrise trek: $35-50 with breakfast
- Yoga retreats: $50-200 per day
- Temple hopping tours: $30-60 full day

Food & Dining:
- Nasi Goreng (fried rice): $2-4
- Babi Guling (roast pork): $3-6
- Fresh seafood BBQ at Jimbaran: $15-30
- Smoothie bowls in Canggu: $5-8
- Fine dining in Seminyak: $30-80 per person

Transportation:
- Scooter rental: $5/day (international license required)
- Private driver: $40-60/day
- Grab/GoJek: $2-10 per trip
- Airport transfers: $15-25

Accommodation Areas:
- Ubud: Culture, rice fields, yoga (budget to luxury)
- Seminyak: Beach clubs, nightlife, shopping (mid to luxury)
- Canggu: Surfers, digital nomads, cafes (budget to mid-range)
- Uluwatu: Surf, cliffs, peaceful (mid to luxury)
- Sanur: Family-friendly, quiet beaches (budget to mid-range)

Tips:
- Rent scooter for flexibility (traffic can be crazy)
- Negotiate prices at markets (expect 30-50% off initial price)
- Avoid tap water, drink bottled/filtered
- Respect temple dress codes (sarong and sash required)
- Get travel insurance for scooter accidents
- Visit waterfalls early morning to avoid crowds
