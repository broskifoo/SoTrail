Tokyo, Japan - Complete Travel Guide

Overview:
Tokyo blends ultra-modern technology with traditional culture. Experience neon-lit streets, ancient temples, world-class cuisine, and efficient public transport.

Best Time to Visit:
March-May (cherry blossoms) or September-November (fall colors). Avoid August (hot, humid, typhoon season).

Budget Breakdown:
- Budget: $60-80/day (hostels, convenience store meals, JR Pass)
- Mid-range: $120-180/day (business hotels, restaurants, attractions)
- Luxury: $300+/day (5-star hotels, fine dining, private experiences)

Top Attractions:
- Senso-ji Temple Asakusa: Ancient Buddhist temple (free entry)
- Shibuya Crossing: World's busiest intersection, iconic photo spot
- Tokyo Skytree: 634m tower with panoramic views ($18-28)
- Meiji Shrine: Peaceful forest shrine in busy Harajuku (free)
- Tsukiji Outer Market: Fresh seafood, street food breakfast

Must-Visit Neighborhoods:
- Shibuya: Youth culture, shopping, nightlife, famous crossing
- Shinjuku: Skyscrapers, entertainment, Golden Gai bars
- Harajuku: Fashion, cosplay, Takeshita Street shopping
- Asakusa: Traditional temples, kimono rentals, old Tokyo
- Akihabara: Anime, manga, electronics, gaming arcades

Food Experiences:
- Conveyor belt sushi: $15-30
- Ramen shops: $8-12 per bowl
- Izakaya (Japanese pub): $25-40 per person
- Convenience store meals: $5-10 (surprisingly good!)
- Michelin star dining: $50-300+
- Street food in Harajuku: $3-8 per item

Transportation:
- JR Pass (7 days): $280 (unlimited trains)
- Single subway ride: $2-4
- Day pass: $8 (Tokyo Metro)
- Taxi: Very expensive, avoid unless necessary
- Suica/Pasmo card: $5 deposit, rechargeable

Day Trip Ideas:
- Mount Fuji & Hakone: Hot springs, lake views ($100-150 tour)
- Nikko: UNESCO shrines, nature ($30 train fare)
- Kamakura: Great Buddha, beaches ($15 train fare)

Unique Experiences:
- Robot Restaurant show: $50-80
- Teamlab Borderless digital art: $32
- Sumo tournament: $25-200 (Jan, May, Sept)
- Mario Kart street tour: $50-80
- Onsen (hot spring bath): $8-15

Shopping:
- Don Quijote: 24/7 mega discount store
- Uniqlo flagship Ginza: Affordable fashion
- Pokemon Center: Official merchandise
- Daiso: $1.50 store, everything cheap

Tips:
- Get pocket WiFi at airport ($8/day)
- No tipping culture (actually rude to tip)
- Learn basic phrases (arigato, sumimasen)
- Cash is king (many places don't accept cards)
- Train etiquette: no phone calls, no eating
- Tattoos not allowed in many onsen/gyms
